Raised in Douglas, MA, Mars is a practicing interdisciplinary artist working in watercolor and illustration. Mars incorporates elements of her imagination through vibrant color palettes, fantastical scenes, and intricacy. Her work centers around themes of animals, landscapes, and fictional characters within magical worlds. She incorporates bright colors and free-forms to create fantastic characters and spaces. Doing this allows her imagination to be integrated into each piece. Mars' projects depict and explore spontaneity and fantasy through intricacy, color, and pattern. Even though her projects span a variety of media, her interest in flora and fauna narratives remains consistent. Each work begins as a representational rendering of a place or animal. The process of painting connects her to the subject matter, making her feel closer to wildlife. Painting animals in their beauty provides a needed change of perspective from the pain creatures face in this life. It is special to Mars to bring fictional stories from her imagination into the world so she never forgets them and in turn makes them real. Her current work is a series of watercolors that depict different animals that are hidden within foods with natural, floral backgrounds. Representing how their mistreatment is glorified in the real world as well as current issues with food transparency. Her other current work as an illustrator, takes a step away from reality and tells a specific story about a character's life, often within a house. All of her work, no matter the media, depicts the beauty and importance of our world's flora and fauna as well as her personal connection to it. Mars currently lives in Beverly, MA, where she maintains an active studio practice and is pursuing a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Studio Art at Endicott College.
